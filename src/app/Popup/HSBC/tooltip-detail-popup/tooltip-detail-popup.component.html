CMR Score

Transunion CIBIL is a leading authorized bureau of India with more than 90% share of market. CIBIL Commercial Rank (CMR) score is a risk model built on their proprietary framework and considers 36 months of trended credit behavior patterns, which takes into account the Credit Demographics, Liquidity Profile, Periodicity and Repayment Behavior of the Borrower. CMR risk differentiates on a scale of CMR-1 to CMR-10, where CMR-1 has the least default probability and CMR-10 has the maximum.

Exclusions from Bureau CMR Score:

(1) Borrowers having an aggregate credit exposure of greater than INR 50 crores in any of the last 36 months

(2) CMR is not applicable for Self Help Groups (SHG), Association of Persons (AOP) and Business entities created by a statute.

Wallet %

Wallet % represents HSBC's share in a customer's total lending exposure reported to the Bureau.

Utilization Wallet % indicates HSBC's outstanding utilisation as a percentage of the customer's total utilised limits across all lenders.

Sanction Wallet %indicates HSBC's sanctioned limits as a percentage of the customer's total sanctioned limits across all lenders.

Please note that exposures to Governments, Other Banks / Financial Institutions, and HSBC group entities (available in E&T files) are not reported to the Bureau and therefore do not form part of this analysis.
